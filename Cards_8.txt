What is a standard Brownian motion on [0,1] (Definition 8.1)?	A process B=(B_t) with (1) B0=0 a.s., (2) a.s. continuous paths, (3) B_t−B_s ~ N(0,t−s), (4) independent increments on disjoint intervals.
In Definition 8.1, what does “stationary increments” mean?	The law of B_t−B_s depends only on (t−s), not on s,t separately.
In Definition 8.1, what does “independent increments” mean?	Increments over disjoint time intervals are independent random variables.
In Prop 8.2, what is E[B_t] for Brownian motion on [0,1]?	E[B_t]=0 for all t∈[0,1].
How does Prop 8.2 show t↦E[B_t] is constant?	E[B_t−B_s]=0 implies E[B_t]=E[B_s] for all s<t.
In Prop 8.2, what is the covariance E[B_s B_t]?	E[B_sB_t]=min(s,t).
In Prop 8.2, which decomposition is used for t≥s?	B_t = B_s + (B_t−B_s).
Why does E[B_s(B_t−B_s)] = 0 in Prop 8.2?	(B_t−B_s) is independent of B_s and has mean 0.
What is Var(B_s) for Brownian motion?	Var(B_s)=s (since B_s ~ N(0,s)).
What key “entry point” fact about Brownian motion is stated in §8.1?	It is a centered Gaussian process with covariance min(s,t).
What does Prop 8.3 assume about B besides Gaussianity to conclude it is Brownian motion?	B0=0 a.s., a.s. continuity, centered Gaussian, and covariance E[B_sB_t]=min(s,t).
In Prop 8.3, what is Var(B_t−B_s) computed to be?	Var(B_t−B_s)=t−s.
Why does Var(B_t−B_s)=t−s imply B_t−B_s ~ N(0,t−s) in Prop 8.3?	Linear combinations of Gaussian vectors are Gaussian, so the increment is Gaussian with that variance.
In Prop 8.3, how is independence of disjoint increments obtained?	Disjoint increments have covariance 0; for jointly Gaussian variables, zero covariance ⇒ independence.
What is the marginal law of B_t stated in Chapter 8?	B_t ~ N(0,t).
How are finite-dimensional distributions described in Chapter 8?	(B_t1,...,B_tm) is multivariate normal as a linear transform of independent Gaussian increments.
What is the increment notation Δ_j used for finite-dimensional distributions?	Δ_j := B_{t_j} − B_{t_{j-1}} with t0:=0.
State the scaling property given in Chapter 8.	For c>0, \tilde B_t := (1/√c) B_{ct} is again standard Brownian motion (on its time domain).
State the symmetry property given in Chapter 8.	(−B_t)_{t∈[0,1]} has the same law as (B_t)_{t∈[0,1]}.
What “regularity” fact about paths is stated before the construction?	Brownian paths are a.s. nowhere differentiable.
What Hölder regularity is stated (quantitatively) for Brownian paths?	For every α∈(0,1/2), paths are a.s. α-Hölder on [0,1].
What Hölder exponent is stated to be impossible a.s.?	No exponent α ≥ 1/2 is possible almost surely.
List one standard construction approach for Brownian motion mentioned in §8.2.	Kolmogorov extension + a continuity theorem (continuous modification).
List another standard construction approach for Brownian motion mentioned in §8.2.	Lévy–Ciesielski (Haar–Schauder) explicit random series.
List a third standard construction approach for Brownian motion mentioned in §8.2.	Invariance principles (scaling limits of random walks).
What covariance characterization is emphasized as the construction target?	Build a centered Gaussian process with E[B(s)B(t)] = min(s,t).
What “guiding principle” is used to build the process in §8.2?	Expand the indicators 1_[0,t] in an orthonormal basis of L^2([0,1]).
What is the general L^2 expansion formula stated for h∈L^2([0,1])?	h = Σ_{k,n} ⟨h,g_{k,n}⟩ g_{k,n}.
What identity ensures inner products are preserved by the expansion?	Parseval’s identity.
What “formal” random-series form for B(t) is suggested before choosing Haar?	B(t)= Σ_{k,n} Z_{k,n} ∫_0^t g_{k,n}(u)du with i.i.d. N(0,1) coefficients.
Why is the Haar basis called “particularly natural” here?	Haar functions are localized on dyadic intervals and their integrals are continuous tent functions.
State the theorem number for the Lévy–Ciesielski/Haar–Schauder construction.	Theorem 8.4.
What does Theorem 8.4 guarantee exists?	A probability space and a process B on [0,1] satisfying the Brownian motion axioms.
What series representation for B(t) is given in Theorem 8.4?	B(t)= Σ_{n=0}^∞ Σ_{k=1}^{2^n} Z_{k,n} f_{k,n}(t).
In Theorem 8.4, how is f_{k,n}(t) defined from Haar functions?	f_{k,n}(t) = ∫_0^t g_{k,n}(u)du.
How many proof “steps” are outlined for Theorem 8.4?	Four steps: basis definition, uniform convergence, covariance identification, Brownian verification.
How is the Haar basis element g_{k,n}(t) defined?	g_{k,n}(t)=2^{n/2}(1_{I^ℓ_{k,n}}(t) − 1_{I^r_{k,n}}(t)).
What is the dyadic interval I_{k,n}?	I_{k,n}=[(k−1)2^{−n}, k2^{−n}).
What is stated about the shape/support of each f_{k,n}?	Continuous, piecewise linear, supported on I_{k,n}.
What sup-norm bound is given for f_{k,n}?	∥f_{k,n}∥_∞ ≤ 2^{-(n/2+1)}.
How are the partial sums B_N(t) defined in Theorem 8.4?	B_N(t)= Σ_{n=0}^N Σ_{k=1}^{2^n} Z_{k,n} f_{k,n}(t).
Why is each B_N a continuous process?	It is a finite sum of continuous functions f_{k,n}(t).
What is the “level-n contribution” Δ_n(t)?	Δ_n(t)= Σ_{k=1}^{2^n} Z_{k,n} f_{k,n}(t).
What disjointness property is used for fixed n?	The supports of {f_{k,n}}_{k=1}^{2^n} are disjoint.
Give the key bound on sup_t |Δ_n(t)| used for uniform convergence.	sup_{t∈[0,1]}|Δ_n(t)| ≤ max_k |Z_{k,n}| · ∥f_{k,n}∥_∞ ≤ 2^{-(n/2+1)} max_k|Z_{k,n}|.
Which probabilistic tools are named to control max_k|Z_{k,n}|?	Gaussian tail estimates + union bound.
Which lemma is used to conclude finiteness of Σ sup_t|Δ_n(t)| a.s.?	Borel–Cantelli lemma.
What conclusion is drawn from Σ sup_t|Δ_n(t)| < ∞ a.s.?	Σ Δ_n(t) converges uniformly in t a.s.
How is the limiting process B(t) defined after uniform convergence?	B(t)= Σ_{n=0}^∞ Δ_n(t).
Why does uniform convergence imply B has continuous sample paths?	Uniform limit of continuous functions on [0,1] is continuous.
Why is B(t) Gaussian for each fixed t?	B_N(t) is a finite linear combo of independent Gaussians; B_N(t)→B(t) in L^2, so B(t) is Gaussian.
What covariance formula is obtained before applying Parseval?	E[B(s)B(t)] = Σ_{n,k} f_{k,n}(s) f_{k,n}(t).
How is f_{k,n}(t) related to the L^2 inner product with g_{k,n}?	f_{k,n}(t)=∫_0^t g_{k,n}(u)du = ⟨1_[0,t], g_{k,n}⟩_{L^2}.
Which identity is used to compute E[B(s)B(t)] from these inner products?	The (bilinear) Parseval identity.
What does Parseval yield for the covariance?	E[B(s)B(t)] = ⟨1_[0,s], 1_[0,t]⟩_{L^2} = ∫_0^{min(s,t)} 1 du = min(s,t).
Given (B(s),B(t)) jointly Gaussian, what is the law of B(t)−B(s)?	B(t)−B(s) ~ N(0, t−s).
How is independence of disjoint increments verified in Step 5?	Show Cov(B(t)−B(s), B(v)−B(u))=0 for disjoint intervals, then use Gaussianity ⇒ independence.
How is B0 handled in the construction?	B0=0 holds trivially from the series definition.
What practical approximation message is stated in Remark 8.5?	Truncating the series gives continuous random paths converging uniformly almost surely.
In one sentence, why does Theorem 8.4 “identify” the constructed process as Brownian motion?	It has continuous paths, Gaussian increments with variance t−s, and independent increments—matching Definition 8.1.
