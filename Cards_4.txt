What is the goal of filtering in Chapter 4?	Estimate the hidden state θ_t using the observation history.
What is the observation filtration F_t^ξ?	F_t^ξ = σ(ξ_0, ξ_1, …, ξ_t).
What are the two processes in the filtering setup?	An unobserved state θ_t and an observation/measurement ξ_t.
Write the state equation (model) for θ_{t+1}.	θ_{t+1}=a0(t)+a1(t)θ_t+b1(t)ε1(t+1)+b2(t)ε2(t+1).
Write the observation equation (model) for ξ_{t+1}.	ξ_{t+1}=A0(t)+A1(t)θ_t+B1(t)ε1(t+1)+B2(t)ε2(t+1).
What are ε1(t) and ε2(t) assumed to be?	i.i.d. standard Gaussian vectors, independent across time and mutually independent.
What measurability/adaptedness is assumed for the coefficients?	a0,a1,b1,b2,A0,A1,B1,B2 are F_t^ξ-measurable (can depend on past observations).
What is the conditional mean m(t)?	m(t)=E[θ_t | F_t^ξ].
What is the conditional covariance Y(t)?	Y(t)=Cov(θ_t | F_t^ξ).
What is meant by “conditionally Gaussian sequence” here?	For each t, the conditional law of θ_t given F_t^ξ is (a.s.) Gaussian.
Why do (m(t), Y(t)) summarize the full conditional law of θ_t?	A Gaussian distribution is determined by its mean and covariance.
What is the key step from time t to t+1 in filtering?	Update from the prior (given F_t^ξ) to the posterior (given F_{t+1}^ξ=σ(F_t^ξ, ξ_{t+1})).
What is the “innovation” ν_{t+1}?	ν_{t+1}=ξ_{t+1}−E[ξ_{t+1} | F_t^ξ].
What is the prediction formula for E[θ_{t+1} | F_t^ξ]?	E[θ_{t+1} | F_t^ξ]=a0(t)+a1(t)m(t).
What is the prediction formula for E[ξ_{t+1} | F_t^ξ]?	E[ξ_{t+1} | F_t^ξ]=A0(t)+A1(t)m(t).
Express ν_{t+1} using coefficients and m(t).	ν_{t+1}=ξ_{t+1}−A0(t)−A1(t)m(t).
Write the state prediction error θ_{t+1}−E[θ_{t+1}|F_t^ξ].	a1(t)(θ_t−m(t))+b1(t)ε1(t+1)+b2(t)ε2(t+1).
Write the observation prediction error ξ_{t+1}−E[ξ_{t+1}|F_t^ξ].	A1(t)(θ_t−m(t))+B1(t)ε1(t+1)+B2(t)ε2(t+1).
Define d11^ξ(t)=Cov(θ_{t+1} | F_t^ξ).	d11^ξ(t)=a1(t)Y(t)a1(t)^T + b1(t)b1(t)^T + b2(t)b2(t)^T.
Define d22^ξ(t)=Cov(ξ_{t+1} | F_t^ξ).	d22^ξ(t)=A1(t)Y(t)A1(t)^T + B1(t)B1(t)^T + B2(t)B2(t)^T.
Define d12^ξ(t)=Cov(θ_{t+1}, ξ_{t+1} | F_t^ξ).	d12^ξ(t)=a1(t)Y(t)A1(t)^T + b1(t)B1(t)^T + b2(t)B2(t)^T.
What theorem powers the Kalman update in these notes?	Gaussian conditioning / normal correlation (Gaussian regression).
State the Gaussian regression mean formula with pseudo-inverse.	E[X|Z]=m_X + Σ_XZ Σ_ZZ^+ (Z−m_Z) for jointly Gaussian (X,Z).
State the Gaussian regression covariance formula with pseudo-inverse.	Cov(X|Z)=Σ_XX − Σ_XZ Σ_ZZ^+ Σ_ZX.
What is Σ_ZZ^+?	The Moore–Penrose pseudo-inverse of Σ_ZZ.
Why do we need a pseudo-inverse in filtering?	The conditional covariance of observations can be singular (redundant/noiseless information).
What is the generalized Kalman gain K(t)?	K(t)=d12^ξ(t) (d22^ξ(t))^+.
Write the generalized Kalman mean update m(t+1).	m(t+1)=E[θ_{t+1}|F_t^ξ] + K(t) ν_{t+1}.
Write the generalized Kalman covariance update Y(t+1).	Y(t+1)=d11^ξ(t) − d12^ξ(t)(d22^ξ(t))^+ d12^ξ(t)^T.
What is the “prior” at time t+1?	The conditional law of θ_{t+1} given F_t^ξ (before seeing ξ_{t+1}).
What is the “posterior” at time t+1?	The conditional law of θ_{t+1} given F_{t+1}^ξ (after seeing ξ_{t+1}).
What does the update do conceptually to the mean?	Prior mean + gain × innovation.
What does the update do conceptually to the covariance?	Subtract the part “explained” by the new measurement (variance reduction).
When does the generalized update reduce to the familiar classical form?	When coefficients/noise covariances are deterministic and the relevant covariance is invertible (no pseudo-inverse needed).
In the classical Kalman filter, what is the standard gain formula?	K=Σ^- H^T (HΣ^-H^T+Σ_R)^{-1}.
In the classical Kalman filter, what is the standard mean update?	x̂ = x̂^- + K(z − Hx̂^-).
In the classical Kalman filter, what is the standard covariance update?	Σ = Σ^- − K(HΣ^-H^T+Σ_R)K^T.
What is the 1D vehicle tracking state vector in the example?	X_k=(p_k, v_k)^T (position, velocity).
What is the sampling interval symbol in the example?	Δ>0.
Write the deterministic kinematics for p_k and v_k with acceleration u.	p_k=p_{k−1}+Δv_{k−1}+(1/2)Δ^2 u_{k−1}; v_k=v_{k−1}+Δu_{k−1}.
How is random acceleration modeled in the example?	Replace u_{k−1} by u_{k−1}+E_{k−1} with E_k~N(0,σ_E^2) i.i.d.
What is the example’s state transition matrix A?	A=[[1,Δ],[0,1]].
What is the example’s process-noise covariance Σ_W (up to σ_E^2 scaling)?	Σ_W=σ_E^2[[ (1/4)Δ^4, (1/2)Δ^3 ],[ (1/2)Δ^3, Δ^2 ]].
What is observed in the example measurement equation?	Only position: z_k = H X_k + R_k with H=[1,0].
What is the measurement noise assumption in the example?	R_k~N(0,σ_R^2).
What is the “core mathematical tool” highlighted in the chapter takeaway?	Gaussian conditioning (normal correlation).
What does “innovation” measure?	The new information in ξ_{t+1} not predictable from past observations.
What does the Kalman gain balance?	How much to trust the new measurement vs the prior uncertainty.
What does “conditionally Gaussianity propagates” mean?	If θ_t|F_t^ξ is Gaussian, then θ_{t+1}|F_{t+1}^ξ is Gaussian as well.
Why does the filtering problem become finite-dimensional here?	The full posterior is summarized by updating only mean and covariance.
What role does measurability of coefficients (F_t^ξ-measurable) play?	It keeps the conditional Gaussian structure valid when conditioning on the observation history.
What is the main reason the pseudo-inverse is acceptable in the update?	The regression/projection formulas remain valid for positive semidefinite (possibly singular) covariance.
