What is the Bellman equation recursion for the value functions?	VN = gN, and for n=0,...,N-1: Vn(x)=sup_{a∈Dn(x)}{ rn(x,a) + ∫_E Vn+1(x') Qn(dx'|x,a) }.
What is the key intuition behind the Bellman equation?	Total value = immediate reward + expected continuation value from the next state.
What is M(E) in Chapter 6?	M(E) := { v:E→[-∞,∞) | v is measurable }.
Define (L_n v)(x,a).	(Lnv)(x,a) := rn(x,a) + ∫_E v(x') Qn(dx'|x,a), for (x,a)∈Dn.
Define (T_n^f v)(x).	(Tn^f v)(x) := (Lnv)(x, f(x)), for x∈E and decision rule f∈Fn.
Define (T_n v)(x).	(Tnv)(x) := sup_{a∈Dn(x)} (Lnv)(x,a), for x∈E.
What is the domain where L_n v is defined?	On admissible state-action pairs (x,a) ∈ Dn ⊂ E×A.
In L_n v, what kernel determines the next-state averaging?	The transition kernel Qn(·|x,a).
What does Remark 6.2(a) say about T_n v?	Tnv = sup_{f∈Fn} Tn^f v.
What does Remark 6.2(b) warn about measurability?	Even if v is measurable, Tn v need not be measurable (sup over uncountable actions can break measurability).
Lemma 6.3: if v≤w pointwise, what inequality holds for L_n?	Ln v(x,a) ≤ Ln w(x,a) for all (x,a)∈Dn.
Lemma 6.3: if v≤w pointwise, what inequality holds for T_n^f?	Tn^f v(x) ≤ Tn^f w(x) for all x and all decision rules f.
Lemma 6.3: if v≤w pointwise, what inequality holds for T_n?	Tn v(x) ≤ Tn w(x) for all x.
Why does v≤w imply ∫ v dQ ≤ ∫ w dQ in Lemma 6.3?	Because Qn(·|x,a) is a probability measure and integration preserves pointwise inequalities.
What is “reward iteration” conceptually?	A backward recursion that computes the value Vn^π of a fixed policy π stage by stage.
Theorem 6.4: what is V_N^π?	VN^π = gN.
Theorem 6.4: what is the recursion for V_n^π?	For n=0,...,N-1: Vn^π = Tn^{f_n} Vn+1^π (if π=(f0,...,fN-1)).
Theorem 6.4: what is the concatenated operator formula for V_n^π?	Vn^π = Tn^{f_n} Tn+1^{f_{n+1}} ··· T_{N-1}^{f_{N-1}} gN.
In Thm 6.4 Step 1, how is Vn^π(x) split?	Immediate reward rn(x,f_n(x)) + expected future rewards from time n+1 onward.
Why can rn(Xn,f_n(Xn)) be replaced by rn(x,f_n(x)) under P_{n,x}^π?	Because Xn=x holds P_{n,x}^π-a.s.
In Thm 6.4, what is the tower property used for?	To condition the future tail reward on Xn+1.
In Thm 6.4, what does the Markov/time-shift step give?	Given Xn+1=x', the conditional expected future reward equals Vn+1^π(x').
Under P_{n,x}^π, what is the law of Xn+1?	Xn+1 ∼ Qn(·|x, f_n(x)).
In reward iteration, what random variable serves as the continuation value?	Vn+1^π(Xn+1).
What does the operator Tn^f represent in words?	“One-step lookahead value” if you choose action f(x) now and then use continuation v.
Section 6.3: what is the intuition behind maximizers?	A maximizer is a decision rule that attains the Bellman supremum (pointwise in the state).
Definition 6.5: when is f a maximizer of v at time n?	If Tn^f v = Tn v.
Definition 6.5: what does maximizer mean pointwise in x?	For each x, f(x) maximizes a↦(Lnv)(x,a) over a∈Dn(x).
Verification theorem: what recursion must (v_n) satisfy?	vN=gN and vn = Tn v_{n+1} for n=0,...,N-1.
Theorem 6.6(a): what bound does a Bellman-satisfying (v_n) provide?	vn ≥ Vn for all n.
Theorem 6.6(b): what extra condition yields vn=Vn and an optimal policy?	Exist maximizers f_n^* of v_{n+1} for every n; then π^*=(f0^*,...,f_{N-1}^*) is optimal.
In the proof of Thm 6.6, what direction is the induction?	Backward induction (from N down to 0).
In Thm 6.6 proof, what property lets you compare Tn vn+1 and Tn Vn+1?	Monotonicity of Tn.
How is any fixed policy π bounded in Thm 6.6 proof?	Vn^π = Tn^{f_n} Vn+1^π ≤ Tn^{f_n} Vn+1 ≤ Tn Vn+1.
Why does taking sup over π matter in Thm 6.6?	Sup_π Vn^π = Vn, so the bound becomes a bound on the optimal value.
How is the candidate optimal policy π^* constructed in Thm 6.6(b)?	Choose maximizers f_n^* at each stage and set π^*=(f0^*,...,f_{N-1}^*).
What is shown by backward induction in Thm 6.6(b) once π^* is defined?	Vn^{π^*} = vn for all n.
Why is a structure assumption needed (Section 6.5 motivation)?	Tn v may be non-measurable and the supremum may fail to be attained without extra assumptions.
SAN (Structure Assumption) item (i): what must hold at terminal time?	gN ∈ MN.
SAN item (ii): what closure property is required?	If v∈M_{n+1}, then Tn v is well-defined and belongs to Mn.
SAN item (iii): what existence property is required?	For every v∈M_{n+1}, there exists a maximizer f_n ∈ Δn.
In SAN, what is Δn a subset of?	Δn ⊂ Fn (a selected class of decision rules).
Structure theorem 6.8(a): what does it conclude about Vn and the Bellman equation?	Vn ∈ Mn for all n and Vn = Tn Vn+1 with VN=gN.
Structure theorem 6.8(b): how can Vn be written as a composition of operators?	Vn = Tn Tn+1 ··· T_{N-1} gN.
Structure theorem 6.8(c): what does it guarantee exists?	Maximizers f_n^* ∈ Δn for Vn+1, and any such sequence yields an optimal policy.
In Thm 6.8 proof, what yields the “upper bound” Vn ≤ Tn Vn+1?	Reward iteration for any π plus monotonicity gives Vn^π ≤ Tn Vn+1, then take sup over π.
How is the “lower bound” Vn ≥ Tn Vn+1 obtained in Thm 6.8?	Use a maximizer f_n^* and an ε-optimal continuation policy from time n+1 onward.
What is an ε-optimal continuation policy (as used in Thm 6.8)?	A policy π^ε such that Vn+1^{π^ε}(x') ≥ Vn+1(x') − ε for each x'.
Which operator property is crucial in the ε-argument of Thm 6.8?	Monotonicity of Tn^{f_n^*}.
Why do we let ε ↓ 0 in Thm 6.8?	To remove the ε-gap and conclude Vn ≥ Tn Vn+1 exactly.
Corollary 6.9 (dynamic programming principle): what is the intermediate-time formula?	For n≤m≤N: Vn(x)=sup_π E_{n,x}^π[ ∑_{k=n}^{m-1} rk(Xk,fk(Xk)) + Vm(Xm) ].
In Corollary 6.9, what role does Vm(Xm) play?	It is the continuation value when stopping the optimization at time m.
Backward induction algorithm (Remark 6.10): what is step 1?	Set VN := gN.
Backward induction algorithm (Remark 6.10): what is step 2?	For n=N-1,...,0 compute Vn(x)=sup_{a∈Dn(x)}{rn(x,a)+∫ Vn+1 dQn} and pick a maximizer f_n^*(x).
What does Remark 6.10 conclude about the policy π^* built from maximizers?	π^*=(f0^*,...,f_{N-1}^*) is optimal.
What phenomenon does Section 6.6 highlight?	A policy can be optimal even if it is not a maximizer at states that are never visited.
Example 6.11: how is the transition kernel defined?	Qn({x'}|x,a) = 1{a = x'} (the action forces the next state).
Example 6.11: what are the rewards rn and g2?	rn(x,a)=a and g2(x)=x.
Example 6.11: why can a policy with a “non-maximizing” action at an unreachable state still be optimal?	Because that state is never visited under the policy, so that action never affects the realized reward.
What is the key message of “stage-wise maximizers everywhere”?	Optimality needs maximization only along states reached with positive probability under the policy.
