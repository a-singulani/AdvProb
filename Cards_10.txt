What is the “setwise viewpoint” for comparing measures?	Compare probabilities of sets: μn(A) vs μ(A).
What is the “test-function viewpoint” for comparing measures?	Compare integrals: ∫ f dμn vs ∫ f dμ.
What is setwise convergence of measures?	μn(A) → μ(A) for every measurable set A.
Why is setwise convergence often “too strong”?	It forces convergence for all events and is usually too rigid for compactness arguments.
What is weak (narrow) convergence?	∫ f dPn → ∫ f dP for all f ∈ Cb(E).
What is vague convergence?	∫ f dPn → ∫ f dP for all f ∈ Cc(E).
Why is vague convergence useful on non-compact spaces?	Compactly supported tests ignore what happens “at infinity”.
What is the intuitive “mass escape” problem on non-compact spaces?	Measures can drift to infinity so weak limits may fail to exist.
What is tightness (informal description in Ch.10 intro)?	“No loss of mass at infinity”: most mass stays in a common compact set uniformly in n.
In Ch.10 setting, what kind of space is (E,d)?	A Polish space.
What is B(E)?	The Borel σ-algebra of E.
