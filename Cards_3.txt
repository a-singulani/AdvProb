What is a filtered probability space (finite horizon)?	(Ω, F, P, (F_n)_{0≤n≤N}) with F_0⊆F_1⊆…⊆F_N⊆F.
What is an adapted process Z=(Z_n)?	Z_n is F_n-measurable for every n.
What is a stopping time T (finite horizon)?	T:Ω→{0,…,N} such that {T=n}∈F_n for all n.
Equivalent stopping time condition using {T≤n}?	{T≤n}∈F_n for all n.
Why require stopping times in optimal stopping?	Decisions must be based only on information available at the time (no “future peeking”).
What is the finite-horizon optimal stopping objective?	Maximize sup_{T≤N} E[Z_T].
What is the key subtlety in optimizing E[Z_T]?	The reward depends on the random time T, so you optimize over random times.
What is the “essential supremum” of random variables?	The smallest (a.s.) random variable that dominates the family, defined up to null sets.
Why use essential supremum in optimal stopping theory?	To define pointwise sup over possibly uncountably many admissible stopping times.
What is the Snell envelope U of Z?	U_n = ess sup_{T≥n} E[Z_T | F_n].
What is the terminal condition for the Snell envelope?	U_N = Z_N.
What is the Snell envelope backward recursion?	U_n = max(Z_n, E[U_{n+1} | F_n]).
Why is U a supermartingale?	E[U_{n+1}|F_n] ≤ U_n by the recursion.
What domination property does the Snell envelope satisfy?	U_n ≥ Z_n for all n.
What minimality property characterizes the Snell envelope?	U is the smallest supermartingale that dominates Z.
What is the “stop vs continue” interpretation of U_n?	U_n is the best achievable expected reward from time n given F_n.
What is the stopping (contact) set at time n?	{U_n = Z_n}.
What is the canonical candidate optimal stopping time T*?	T* = min{n : U_n = Z_n}.
Why is T* a stopping time?	The event {T*≤n} depends only on comparisons up to time n (F_n-measurable).
What value does the Snell envelope give for the optimal objective?	sup_{T≤N}E[Z_T] = E[U_0].
State the supermartingale domination principle.	If Y is a supermartingale with Y_n≥Z_n, then sup_T E[Z_T] ≤ E[Y_0].
How do you prove the domination principle (idea)?	Use optional sampling: E[Y_T] ≤ E[Y_0] and Y_T≥Z_T.
How does the Snell envelope relate to the domination principle?	It achieves equality: E[U_0]=sup_T E[Z_T].
What is Doob decomposition for an integrable adapted supermartingale Y?	Y_n = M_n − A_n with M a martingale and A predictable increasing.
In Doob decomposition, what are the initial conditions?	M_0=Y_0 and A_0=0.
Give the increment formula for A in Doob decomposition.	ΔA_n = Y_{n−1} − E[Y_n | F_{n−1}] ≥ 0.
How can you recover M from (Y,A)?	M_n = Y_n + A_n.
What happens when you apply Doob decomposition to the Snell envelope U?	U = M − A with A increasing and M a martingale.
For the Snell envelope, when can ΔA_n be positive?	Only when it is optimal to stop at time n−1 (the contact set is hit).
What is the “practical martingale recipe” Step 1?	Guess an adapted process Y that dominates Z (candidate value process).
Recipe Step 2: what must be verified?	Y is a supermartingale and Y_n≥Z_n for all n.
Recipe Step 3: how define a candidate optimal time?	T = min{n : Y_n = Z_n}.
Recipe Step 4: what proves optimality?	Show E[Y_T]=E[Y_0] and Y_T=Z_T, giving E[Z_T]=E[Y_0].
What is the Markovian optimal stopping setup?	Z_n = g_n(X_n) where (X_n) is a Markov chain.
What is the Markovian value function V_n(x)?	V_n(x)=sup_{T≥n} E[g_T(X_T) | X_n=x].
What is the Markovian Bellman recursion for stopping?	V_N=g_N and V_n(x)=max(g_n(x), ∫ V_{n+1}(x') Q(dx'|x)).
What does the “continue value” represent in the recursion?	The expected next-step value if you do not stop now.
What is the Markovian optimal stopping rule (form)?	Stop when g_n(X_n)=V_n(X_n).
Why does backward induction work in finite-horizon stopping?	Only finitely many times, so recursion from N down to 0 is exact.
What is the key computational output of the Snell envelope recursion?	A value process and a (first-hit) optimal stopping time.
