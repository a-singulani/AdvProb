What is a stochastic process with state space E and index set T (Def 7.1)?	A family (X_t)_{t∈T} of E-valued random variables, i.e., measurable maps X_t:(Ω,F)→(E,B(E)) for each t.
What is the path space E^T?	The set of all functions ω:T→E (all trajectories).
What is the coordinate projection π_t on E^T?	π_t(ω) := ω(t).
How is the product σ-algebra B(E^T) defined (Remark 7.2)?	The smallest σ-algebra on E^T making every coordinate map π_t measurable.
How can a process (X_t) be viewed as one random element (Remark 7.2)?	As X:Ω→E^T with X(ω)=(X_t(ω))_{t∈T}.
When is X:Ω→E^T measurable (Remark 7.2)?	Iff each coordinate X_t = π_t∘X is measurable.
Why do we focus on finite observations for processes indexed by infinite T?	Because describing the joint law of (X_t)_{t∈T} directly is infeasible; finite-time laws are the accessible building blocks.
What is a finite index set I in this chapter’s notation?	A finite subset I={t1,…,tn}⊂T of time indices.
What is the product space E^I?	The Cartesian product of copies of E indexed by I (equivalently E^n once an order is chosen).
What is the finite-dimensional distribution at times I (Def 7.3)?	μ_I := P ∘ (X_{t1},…,X_{tn})^{-1} on (E^I, B(E^I)).
What does μ_I represent intuitively?	The joint law of the process observed only at times in I.
What is the canonical projection π_{J→I} for I⊂J?	The map π_{J→I}:E^J→E^I that forgets coordinates in J\I.
What does “consistency” of (μ_I) mean (Def 7.4)?	For all finite I⊂J⊂T: μ_I = μ_J ∘ π_{J→I}^{-1}.
What does consistency express in words (Remark 7.5)?	Lower-dimensional marginals come from higher-dimensional laws by projection.
How is marginalization written in this chapter for I⊂J?	μ_J ∘ π_{J→I}^{-1}(A) = μ_J(A × E^{J\I}).
Why is consistency necessary for a process to exist?	Because any actual process automatically satisfies that adding extra coordinates and integrating them out doesn’t change probabilities of events depending only on I.
Why is consistency sufficient (high level)?	Daniell–Kolmogorov shows a consistent family (μ_I) can be “glued” into a measure on path space.
What problem do cylinder sets address?	They give a concrete class of events in E^T that depend only on finitely many coordinates.
What is a cylinder set associated with (I, A_I) (Def 7.7)?	C_{A_I} := {ω∈E^T : (ω(t))_{t∈I} ∈ A_I} = π_I^{-1}(A_I).
How can the cylinder set C_{A_I} be written as a product?	C_{A_I} = A_I × E^{T\I}.
What kind of event does a cylinder set encode?	An event that restricts finitely many time coordinates and ignores the rest.
What is a “special cylinder set” (Def 7.8)?	A finite intersection ⋂_{k=1}^n {ω: ω(t_k)∈B_k} = ⋂_{k=1}^n π_{t_k}^{-1}(B_k).
How are special cylinder sets related to rectangles?	They correspond to rectangular sets B1×…×Bn in finite dimensions.
Why introduce special cylinder sets (Remark 7.9)?	They form a manageable algebra for constructing measures and checking additivity.
What is the collection 𝒜 in Lemma 7.10?	The set of all special cylinder sets.
What does Lemma 7.10 state about 𝒜?	𝒜 is an algebra: contains Ω, closed under complements, and closed under finite unions.
How is Ω a special cylinder set (idea)?	Choose constraints B_k=E (or use an empty intersection).
What is the key σ-algebra identity in Lemma 7.11?	σ(𝒜) = B(E^T).
Why is σ(𝒜) ⊂ B(E^T) immediate?	Special cylinder sets are cylinder sets, and B(E^T) is generated by cylinder sets.
Why is B(E^T) ⊂ σ(𝒜) (main idea)?	Rectangles generate B(E^I), and rectangles correspond to special cylinder sets.
What is the practical takeaway of Lemma 7.11?	Defining a measure on special cylinder sets (consistently) determines it on the whole product σ-algebra.
Why does Daniell–Kolmogorov start with a pre-measure on an algebra?	Because Carathéodory extends σ-additive set functions from an algebra to the generated σ-algebra.
What is “inner regularity” (Lemma 7.13)?	For μ on a Polish space and Borel A: μ(A)=sup{μ(K): K⊂A, K compact}.
What does tightness mean in these notes (Remark 7.14)?	Probability mass can be captured in compact sets; in particular, every probability measure on a Polish space is tight.
Why is tightness important for extension in Step 4 of Thm 7.15?	It prevents “mass escaping to infinity” and helps show σ-additivity of the pre-measure.
State the Daniell–Kolmogorov theorem (Thm 7.15) in one line.	If E is Polish and (μ_I) is consistent, there exists a unique P on (E^T,B(E^T)) with P∘π_I^{-1}=μ_I for all finite I.
In Thm 7.15 Step 1, how is P0 defined on cylinder sets?	For C=π_I^{-1}(A), set P0(C):=μ_I(A).
What must be checked for P0 in Thm 7.15 Step 2?	Well-definedness: the value doesn’t depend on the representation of the same cylinder set.
How does consistency help well-definedness (Step 2 idea)?	If two cylinder descriptions agree, lift both to I∪J and use consistent marginals to show the assigned probabilities match.
What is proved in Thm 7.15 Step 3?	Finite additivity of P0 on disjoint (special) cylinder sets.
What is proved in Thm 7.15 Step 4?	σ-additivity of P0 on the algebra (using tightness/inner regularity in the Polish space).
What theorem is used in Thm 7.15 Step 5 to extend P0?	Carathéodory extension theorem.
What does “uniqueness” in Thm 7.15 mean?	Any two measures on B(E^T) with the same finite-dimensional marginals must be equal.
Why does agreement on cylinder sets imply uniqueness on B(E^T)?	Cylinder sets generate B(E^T).
What is a Lusin space (Def 7.16)?	A measurable space (E,𝓔) measurably isomorphic (bijectively, with measurable inverse) to a Polish space.
How do Polish and Lusin spaces relate (Remark 7.17)?	Every Polish space is Lusin, but not every Lusin space is Polish.
What is Theorem 7.18 (existence in Lusin spaces) in one line?	For Lusin (E,𝓔), any consistent family (μ_I) yields an E-valued process with those finite-dimensional laws.
What is the main proof idea of Thm 7.18?	Push forward μ_I via an isomorphism to a Polish space, apply Daniell–Kolmogorov there, then pull back.
In Thm 7.18, what role does the bijection φ:E→Ē play?	It transports the problem to a Polish space where DK applies.
In Thm 7.18, what is the “canonical process” on E^T?	X_t(ω):=ω(t) under the constructed path-space measure.
What does Remark 7.19 emphasize about applications?	Process existence depends mainly on measurable structure (Lusin), not necessarily on being Polish.
What is the “path-space viewpoint” justified by Daniell–Kolmogorov?	Studying processes via probability measures on trajectory space E^T whose coordinate projections are the process.
How do finite-dimensional distributions connect to cylinder events?	Cylinder events are preimages under π_I of sets in E^I, so their probabilities are determined by μ_I.
If you know P on E^T, how do you recover μ_I?	μ_I = P ∘ π_I^{-1}.
If you know all μ_I consistently, what do you get by DK?	A unique measure P on (E^T,B(E^T)) realizing them as marginals.
What is the core compatibility requirement across “resolutions” of observation?	Distributions at fewer times must be the marginals of distributions at more times (consistency).
Why aren’t arbitrary subsets of E^T practical to assign probabilities to directly?	E^T is infinite-dimensional; cylinder sets provide the manageable finite-coordinate events.
What is the dual role of cylinder sets mentioned in the text?	They are (i) a concrete class to define probabilities from μ_I and (ii) generators of the product σ-algebra.
How does Lemma 7.11 help the extension argument?	It ensures the σ-algebra generated by the algebra you start with is the full B(E^T).
What is the key technical obstacle to proving σ-additivity of P0 from finite additivity?	Showing continuity for decreasing sequences / preventing loss of mass at infinity.
What property of Polish spaces is used to control that obstacle?	Inner regularity (hence tightness) of probability measures on B(E).
